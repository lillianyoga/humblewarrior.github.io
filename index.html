About
Humble Warrior

Humble Warrior, or Virabhdrasana in Sanskrit, is a pose named after Virabhadra, a powerful warrior in Hindu mythology. Vira means hero and bradra means friend. This warrior represents the destruction of evil, ignorance and delusion.

We named our business Humble Warrior because a warrior, to us, represents strength and bravery… and a Humble Warrior is a warrior who knows where they come from.

To us, yoga is not just a physical practice on a mat. It is meant to be something you take with you, and shared with your community.

October 6-9, 2023
New Lebanon, New York

Join us at this 18th century farm set on 50 acres in the Berkshires, 30 minutes outside Albany, Indigenous Peoples Weekend, October 6-9, 2023.

The space consists of 5 separate buildings, a willow-lined pond, forest, and farmland. The buildings include a Carriage House. a Farmhouse, a Creamery, a Chalet-style loft apartment, and a second loft apartment.

Every day starts with a yoga class followed by brunch. In the afternoons, we will hike, explore the beautiful grounds, and visit the nearby Norman Rockwell Museum. The itinerary includes pranayama, meditation, yoga philosophy, vinyasa, iyengar, and meridian yoga classes, yet also leaves plenty of time to laze on the grounds, soak in the hot tub, and just be. We will have the whole place to ourselves. Delicious, thoughtfully prepared meals will be provided by Alysia Lozado.

Price includes room, all meals, yoga, and all excursions. Price for a private room: $1750; for shared accommodation: $1250. Book by August 1 for 10% discount. Pay in full by August 1 for 15% discount.

February 1-7, 2024
Rishikesh & New Delhi, India

We are going to India! Join us!

We will spend a week split between Rishikesh, a city renowed as a center for studing yoga and meditation, and Delhi, a cosmopolitan modern day city. The itinerary will be a blend of city and country, old and new, traditional and modern.

In Rishikesh, we will stay at the Tarang Yoga Ashran, where we will enjoy 3 meals a day, and at least two yoga classes a day -- meditation, philosophy, asana -- some taught by us, and some taught by the specialists who live at the ashran. We will swim in the Ganges, go hiking, and explore the caves there. There will be plenty of time for you to also book ayurvedic treatments or massages with the staff at the ashran, as well as simply just bask in the beautiful spiritual surroundings.

In Delhi, we will stay at the Poshtel Hotel, a lovely place in the Hauz Khas neighborhood. We will explore the city one day, have an evening to taste the varity of street food at a local market, and spend another evening at a traditional sit-down restaurant. We also have a full day excursion to Agra to see the Taj Mahal, ending with a bar crawl back in Delhi. There will also be meditation and yoga every day, as well as free time to go shopping, or explore on your own.

Price includes room, all meals, all yoga, and all excursions. Massages and ayurvedic treatments in Rishikesh would be an optional additional cost. Travel between the two cities is included, but travel to and from India is not. Price for a private room: $2000; for shared accommodation: $1500. Pay in full by October 1 for 10% discount.

Shane Allen

Shane is an experienced yoga teacher (E-RYT) with a speciality in meridian yoga therapy, a certified Pilates mat instructor, and an Acu Detox Specialist (ADS) working in harm reduction for the city's most overlooked neighbors. Additionally, he’s continuing his study at Hunter College as well as the AASECT recognized Sexual Health Alliance (SC). Shane originally found his way to yoga as a way to cope with chronic panic attacks. Having just moved to the city for acting school, he’d learned the body tells a story. Yoga taught him to listen. In his classes, Shane hopes to charm his students to meet challenges with honor, humility, and humor.

Lillian Cuthbert

Lillian found yoga at an early age and immediately recognized its many benefits. She fell in love with the practice and the way it enhanced her life both physically and mentally. She completed the 200-hour training at Yoga Vida in 2018 and further studied under the guidance of her mentor, Sangeeta Vallabhan. She currently has a E-RYT 500 Yoga Alliance designation.

Lillian’s classes are athletic and challenging, yet balanced, safe, and accessible to all. She believes that a mindful yoga practice extends beyond the mat and infuses everything we do. With that in mind, Lillian aims to have people leave her classes feeling stronger, calmer, and more connected to the world around them.

In 2018, Lillian climbed Mount Everest, completing the Seven Summits. She has run the New York Marathon ten times, has qualified for and run the Boston Marathon twice. She is the treasurer of The Practice Coalition, a non-profit dedicated to providing yoga for trauma to survivors of domestic and sexual violence and to taking care of the teachers who offer it. She is also the co-founder, with Shane Allen, of Humble Warrior, through which she co-organizes and co-leads retreats and teacher trainings around the globe. She holds a PhD in Electrical Engineering.
